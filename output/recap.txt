🌍 MARKET NEWS RECAP

**🌍 MARKET OVERVIEW**

Global markets saw a mixed session today as US futures pulled back slightly, with the S&P 500 futures down 0.08% ahead of key labor market data. European stocks were largely flat, pausing after a strong rally, as the Stoxx Europe 600 gained 0.1%, while Germany's DAX climbed 0.5%. In China, the Shanghai Composite Index edged higher by 0.3% to its highest level since July 2015, supported by expectations of central bank liquidity measures. The ADP employment report showed private sector job creation came in below consensus, helping to push US Treasury yields lower.

**💼 PORTFOLIO FOCUS**

The technology sector remained a key driver of momentum, with the semiconductor and data storage industries seeing significant gains from the previous session continuing the AI optimism. Memory chip stocks like Micron and Western Digital (WDC) posted double-digit and strong upward rallies respectively, fueled by CES 2026 news and memory chip shortage concerns. Companies Lam Research (LRCX) and Applied Materials (AMAT) also saw gains exceeding 4%. Conversely, energy stocks faced headwinds as crude oil prices eased following reports of an agreement for the US to sell sanctioned Venezuelan crude, increasing global supply. Investors are now focused on upcoming US labor market data, including the JOLTS Job Openings and Friday's payrolls report, to gauge the Federal Reserve's monetary policy path.
