🌍 MARKET NEWS RECAP

Global markets traded mixed, driven by a rotation out of mega-cap technology and into cyclical and defense sectors. US indices closed with the S&P 500 flat and the Nasdaq Composite falling 0.44%, while the Dow Jones rose 0.55% on Thursday. In Asia, the Shanghai Composite advanced 0.9% after official data showed China's consumer inflation picked up in December to its fastest pace in nearly three years, suggesting improved domestic demand. European markets were mixed, with the Euro Stoxx 50 closing down 0.32% on Thursday, although morning trade saw a modest recovery as investors digested positive Eurozone unemployment data.

💼 PORTFOLIO FOCUS

The technology sector faced continued pressure, with high-growth AI-linked stocks seeing profit-taking and a rotation of capital; NVDA shares, for example, fell over 2% amid renewed scrutiny over AI-related capital expenditures and talk of limits on high-end chip orders. In contrast, the defense sector saw a strong rally, with names like Lockheed Martin and Northrop Grumman posting significant gains on speculation of a substantial increase to the US military budget. The basic materials sector was a major focus in Europe following reports of potential mega-merger talks between Rio Tinto and Glencore, sending Glencore shares up 7.5% in morning trading. This sectoral shift drove the mixed performance across the US market, with the S&P 500 barely moving while the Nasdaq underperformed. The Shanghai Composite, however, was boosted by the domestic inflation data.
