🌍 MARKET NEWS RECAP

**1. 🌍 MARKET OVERVIEW**

US equities closed mixed as investors rotated out of high-flying technology stocks, though the S&P 500 still managed to hit a new all-time high on the back of a resilient jobs report. The Nasdaq Composite fell 0.44% as investors digested new rate-cut timing expectations and a cooling in the AI hardware trade. In Europe, the Euro Stoxx 50 closed slightly lower, down 0.3%, as markets took a breather after setting record highs earlier in the week. Conversely, the Shanghai Composite rallied sharply, climbing 0.9% to close at a 10.5-year high following positive domestic inflation data suggesting an improvement in demand.

**2. 💼 PORTFOLIO FOCUS**

The AI hardware trade saw a notable deceleration, with Nvidia (NVDA) falling over 2% amid cooling sentiment and new talks about potential limits on H200 chip orders. Financials are in focus as JPMorgan Chase (JPM) prepares to kick off bank earnings next week, with the bank's results expected to test the "soft landing" narrative. JPMorgan's report will also carry a 2.2 billion provision for credit losses related to its forward purchase commitment of the Apple (AAPL) Card portfolio. Meanwhile, Tesla (TSLA) was a strong performer, gaining nearly 2% as capital rotated into cyclicals and away from the dominant technology names. Microsoft (MSFT) remains a key stock to watch as the market scrutinizes guidance on AI infrastructure spending during the upcoming tech earnings season.
