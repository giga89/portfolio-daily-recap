🌍 MARKET NEWS RECAP

**1. 🌍 MARKET OVERVIEW**

US indices closed mixed as a rotation out of high-growth technology continued, leaving the Nasdaq Composite down 0.44% while the S&P 500 was marginally higher. European markets, including the Euro Stoxx 50, traded slightly lower by about 0.3% as investors cooled off after recent record highs and digested fresh geopolitical news. In Asia, the Shanghai Composite was a strong performer, climbing 0.9% following data showing China's December inflation rate picked up to a near three-year high, signaling a modest recovery in domestic demand. International crude oil prices continued their rebound, accelerated by geopolitical supply disruption concerns, with WTI crude closing up over 3.6%.

**2. 💼 PORTFOLIO FOCUS**

Nvidia was under pressure, falling over 2% yesterday, as the broader AI hardware trade cooled and investors rotated into other sectors. The company announced the hiring of its first Chief Marketing Officer, Alison Wagonfeld, signaling a strategic push to boost brand awareness beyond the core tech space. Microsoft remains a key player in the AI infrastructure war, with the market monitoring its massive capital expenditure and the planned ramp-up of its custom Maia 200 and Cobalt 100 silicon to reduce reliance on third-party chips. Chinese tech giant Alibaba surged more than 5% on news that China is planning to approve certain imports of advanced H200 chips, a significant catalyst that supports the company’s aggressive domestic AI development efforts.
